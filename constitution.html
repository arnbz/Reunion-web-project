Constitution of Thikana 86




Chapter ONE: Preamble


1.1 The bond of our friendship amongst those who passed out in 1986 was borne in the sweet corridor MOTIJHEEL GOVT HIGH SCHOOL. The bond continues and triggers a dream to foster the time-tested relation within a framework of family, society and institutional association. This is the origin of THIKANA-86 - a social, voluntary , non-political and non-religious association. 


1.2 THIKANA-86 aims to capitalize the existing bond of friendship in furtherance of a tie of unity to support each other in every sphere of life


1.3 This association will act as the nucleus

- To recall good old memories and arrange social ties.

- To enable us to groom up own families with cohesion, values and ethos

- To unite and support each other.


1.4 THIKANA with the core motto ‘We grow, care and support’ pleading to grow as a good citizen of the country, enhance care amongst families of all the stakeholders and support each other whenever needed. We affirm our sacred duty towards the association and make our full socio-cultural contribution towards progressive aspirations of our family, society and the country. 


Name:THIKANA 86

Location:The Head office of the Association shall be located at Dhaka.

Address: House 12, Road 02, Sector 03 UTTARA, Dhaka


Objectives:The objectives shall be:

a. To maintain and foster the time tested bond of friendship amongst the students of Motijheel Govt High school (86 Batch).


b. To act as a link for social communication, exchanging ideas and social get- together.


c. To extend financial support for the children of the members in case of inability to continue bare minimum education.


d. To extend co-operation for the family members of the association for the treatment of critical disease.


e. To support grooming up of the family peers with values, ethos and enhance family ties amongst the stakeholders.


f. To keep a complete record of all ex-students of Motijheel Govt High School (86 Batch) including all Members, Associated Members and Honorary Members. 


g. To participate in the events of charity through donation, medical, educational, social and moral support to the people who is in dire need of support.


1.5 THIKANA shall remain a social and voluntary organization and shall not indulge into any political and religious activity.


1.6-1.9 Reserved


ARTICLE TWO: DEFINITIONS


2.1 The definitions are as follows: 


i) The name of the Association shall be “THIKANA-86. 


ii) The association means the association for ex-students of MOTIJHEEL GOVT HIGH SCHOOL (1986 Batch). 


iii) “Members” means General Members, Associated Members and Honorary Members unless otherwise specified. 


iv) President, Vice-President, Secretary General, Central Committee Members means the President, Vice-President, Secretary General and Members of the Central Committee of the Association 


v) “Constitution” means the Constitution of the Association including the amendments made from time to time. 


vi) “Batch” means the passed out Batches of MOTJHEET GOVT HIGH SCHOOL (Both Boys and Girls Section).  


vii) ‘Family Members’ include the Member of the Association, his/ her spouse and children. Family members are by default the Associated Members. 


viii) ‘Critical Disease’ mean those which are detrimental to life saving. 


ix) ‘Bare minimum Education’ extends up to the education in College. 


2.2-2.9 Reserved 


ARTICLE THREE: MEMBERSHIP 


3.1 General Member 


All ex students of MOTIJHEEL GOVT HIGH SCHOOL of 1986 Batch can be a General Member provided that: 


i) He subscribes regularly. 

ii) Abides by the rules and regulation of the Association.  


3.2 Associate Member 


i) The wives/husbands and children of the General Members are by default Associate Members of the Association. 


ii) Any ex students of MOTIJHEEL GOVT HIGH SCHOOL other than 1986 Batch, if willing to join the Association, may do so as an Associate Member. 


iii) The Associate Members shall have no voting rights but their good suggestions are humbly solicited. 


iv) The Central Committee shall decide upon the facilities and responsibilities that may be provided to the Associate Members by the Association. 


3.3 Honorary Member 


i) Any person who has rendered outstanding service to the Association may be awarded with honorary membership of the Association by a majority decision ofthe Central Committee.    


ii) Teachers of the School. 


iii) Any Honorary member shall be eligible to enjoy all the facilities extended to theGeneral Members of the Association but will have no voting rights. 


iv) The Honorary Members shall not be required to pay subscriptions to the Association. However, his/ her voluntary contribution (if any) will be appreciated always. 


3.4 General Membership Ruling 


i) Once registered, a member shall ever remain a General Member if he has not lost his membership or has not proved to be unworthy of a member. 


ii) Permanent loss of membership shall be affected upon those who fail to abide by the rules or code of conduct of the Association, or has been expelled his/her membership by a General Meeting for disciplinary or unethical reason.  


iii) Any member who does not pay his subscription for three consecutive years temporarily loses his membership. In case of temporary loss he retains his primary membership and may revive his membership by paying all past dues. The Central Committee in special circumstances may exempt the past dues of the member and in that case he shall have to pay membership renewal fee. 


3.5 Withdrawal of Membership 


Any member may withdraw his membership through written notice to the CC. In case of a withdrawal, the member losses his General Membership. The same individual may apply for a new membership. 


3.6 Removal, Expulsion of a Member 


i) Any membership may be expelled only by the decision of the General Meeting on events contravening the Association or discipline or in the interest of the society or any other ground which the meeting decides reasonable. 


ii) When it is difficult to hold a General Meeting, the CC shall have the authority to suspend the membership of any member on reasonable grounds after the member has been notified with a 30 days call for explanation and provided that such an action is also approved by a 2 /3 majority of the Central Committee.  


3.7 Voting Rights 


i) Each general member shall have equal voting status. To be a voter in any voting occasion, the member must have his dues cleared for last 3 consecutive years.  


ii) Honorary / Associated Members will have no voting rights although their right to suggest/ comment is always preserved.


3.8 Membership Fee/Subscription  


i) To be a General Member of THIKANA-86, each eligible person shall have to register himself as a General member by paying the Registration Fee Taka 3000 (Taka three thousand only). 


ii) To be an Associated Member (Not being the spouse/ children of a General member), each eligible person shall have to register himself as an Associated Member by paying the Registration Fee Taka 2000 (Taka two thousand only).


iii) An Honorary/ Associated Member (being the spouse/ children of a General Member) are not obliged to pay any subscription/ Registration Fee.  


iv) The Annual Subscription for all General Member is Taka 3600 (Taka Three Thousand Six Hundred only). The minimum limit of the Annual Fee is Taka 100 per month. The maximum subscription has ‘No Limit’; it is totally voluntary and would be highly appreciated. 


v) The amount of the Fee/ Subscription shall be decided/ amended by the Central Committee in a General Meeting where a 2/3 majority concurs the same. 


vi) Any voluntary donation, subscription by any voluntary/ charity organization/ individual is always appreciated for the good and noble cause. 


vii) General Membership Renewal Fee Taka 5000 (Taka Five thousand only) shall be charged in case of renewal of a suspended membership. 


3.9 Exemption of Dues/ Annual Subscription  


The Central Committee may exempt any member from paying his/ her annual subscription in special cases of insolvency for any particular period. In such case, the concerned member shall furnish a written application to the Central Committee. The duration of such exemption shall be decided by the Central Committee.  


ARTICLE FOUR: THE CENTRAL COMMITTEE   


4.1 The Central Committee of THIKANA-86 shall consist of- 


i) President 

ii) Vice-President 

iii) Secretary General 

iv) Treasurer 

v) Member Secretary 

vi) Public Relation and Records Coordinator 

vii) Cultural Secretary (One representative each from Boys and Girls High school)  

viii) Overseas Coordinator/ Coordinators (preferably a member/members residing abroad) 

ix) Welfare Secretary 


4.2 Central committee may incorporate advisers to suggest/ advise the committee.  


4.3 There cannot be more than two portfolio-holders in the Central Committee from any particular batch in any particular term. 


4.4 Formation 


i) The Central Committee shall be elected for a period of 2 years through an election of the General Members in any General Meeting. 


ii) The Election Commission (a committee of 5 members nominated by the Central Committee) shall conduct the election.  


iii) The candidates for each portfolio shall be nominated by the Central Committee during a General Meeting. 


iv) In case of any vacancy during interim period, the Central Committee can co-opt a member, which has to be approved by the next General Meeting. 


4.5 Functions and Authority 


i) The Central Committee of THIKANA-86 shall be responsible for running the overall activities and administration of the Association. 


ii) The Committee shall have the authority to collect fees, subscriptions, contributions.  


iii) The Central Committee will can decide any expenditure below 5 lacs without holding any General Meeting. However, if the expenditure exceeds the said amount it shall be decided by a General Meeting. 


iv) The Committee may form any Sub Committee/ Trust for the better performance of the Association and its events/ activities. 


v) The Central Committee shall be responsible to the General Members for the overall function of the Association. The Committee shall present the resume of activities and budget during the Annual General Meeting. 


vi) The Committee may circulate guidelines/ Memorandums as deemed necessary for the administration/ conduct of the Association, Sub-committees and incorporate rules or amendments as required by this Constitution. 


4.6 Meeting of the Central Committee 


i) The Central Committee shall meet at least once in every 6 months to review the activities of the Association, of which the second meeting which will be treated as the Annual General Meeting. 


ii) The Meeting of the Central Committee shall be called by the Secretary General in consultation with the President and the Central Committee members. The meeting notification shall be given one month in advance. The agenda shall be notified at least 15 days before the meeting.  


iii) In the absence of the Secretary General, Additional Secretary General shall call the meeting. If both are absent then the President may call the meeting. 


iv) Annual General Meeting shall involve minimum 20 General Members excluding Central Committee Members to fill up the quorum. In any other General Meeting minimum 10 General Members shall make the quorum.  


v) In any general meeting the presence of minimum 7 Central CommitteeMembers is a pre requisite. 


vi) A Special General Meeting arising from an exigency may be called with a 7 days notice. 


4.7 Eligibility to the Central Committee 


Any member of the Association can compete for any portfolio in the Central Committee or be a member of it if, 


i) One has not lost his membership of has not been declared incompetent by a General Meeting for such post. 


ii) One is not competing for the 3rd consecutive term in the same portfolio. 


4.8 Removal of Central Committee Member 


i) Once elected, the member of the Central Committee shall continue for a period of 2 years. 


a) In case of eventualities, any member of the Central Committee may be suspended by two third majority raise a ‘No Confidence Bill’.  


b) For approval, the Central Committee may put the member under suspension till the next General Meeting. 

c) If the General Meeting approves the suspension, the Election Committee elects new member. 


ii) Any elected member of the Central Committee withdraws his/her appointment from the committee through a written resignation to the President. If approved by the Committee, he/she shall be obliged to make proper hand over before leaving the Central Committee. 


4.9 President 


i) The President is the Chief Executive of the Association.  


ii) He is responsible from the overall conduct and management of the Association. 


iii) He will preside over all the Central Committee Meetings and General Meetings of the Association. 


iv) He shall have a right of casting vote in case of a tie. 


v) In case of his absence, the Vice President shall take over his charters. 


4.10 Vice-Presidents 


There will be two Vice Presidents, Vice President-1 (From Boys High School) and Vice-President-2 (From Girls High School). In the absence of the President, The Vice President-1 shall act as the President. 


i )He shall be a forerunner to promote fund generation. 

ii) He shall plan events and meetings. 

iii) He is the deputy to the President 


4.11Secretary General 


i) The Secretary General shall be responsible to the Central Committee and the General Members for the overall management of funds, properties and the activities of the Association during his tenure. 


ii) He shall maintain the accounts along with the Treasurer, records and files of the Association. 


iii) He shall have the authority to sign for the Central Committee in matters pertaining to the administration of the Association. 


iv) He shall call the Central Committee Meetings and General Meetings in consultation with the President. 


He shall prepare a Report for the Annual General Meeting every year containing inter alia: 

The activities performed of the year 

A financial statement of fund position 

The receipts, utilization and disbursement of funds in different accounts. 

A plan for future activities. 


vi) The financial powers and limits of the Secretary General and the Treasurer shall be decided in the meeting of the Central Committee. However, it may be reviewed time to time as may during the General meeting. 


vii) He may delegate some of his duties to the additional Secretary General or any member of the Central Committee for ensuring expedient outcome. 


4.12 Additional Secretary General 


In the absence of the Secretary General, he shall act as the Secretary General and shall be entrusted with the duties, responsibilities and powers of the Secretary General. Regarding election, continuance, cessation or removal, the rules and regulation applied to the Secretary General shall be applicable to the Additional Secretary General. 


4.13 Treasurer 

The Treasurer along with the Secretary General shall be responsible for the management and control of funds of the Association. 


i) He shall collect subscription and donation of the members and keep an account of them. 


ii) He shall maintain and disburse funds and keep up-to date accounts of those funds. 


iii) He shall assist the Secretary General in preparing the Financial Statement concerning and collection, utilization and disbursement of funds to be presented in the General Meeting. 


iv) He shall be one of the joint signatories, along with the Secretary General of the Association Bank Accounts. 


4.14 Public Relations Secretary/ Records Coordinator 


i) He/She shall focus on maintaining communication and rapport amongst the members. 


ii) He/ She shall act as pivot in organizing various social events of the Association. 


iii) He/She shall promote the image and activities of the Association. 


iv) He/She shall maintain close liason with the members through social website, group e-mail and other means. 


v) He/She is to circulate the notice of events to all members. 


vi) He/ She shall keep records of complete information of all members. 


4.15 Overseas Secretary 


i) He/She shall maintain contacts with the members residing abroad in fulfilling the objectives of the Association. 


ii) He/She shall be the ‘Point of Contact’ in monitoring and liaising fund raising/ subscription collection from the members residing abroad. 


iii) He/She shall maintain close liaison with Public Relation Secretary ensure circulation to the members abroad vis a vis Feedback. 


4.16 Cultural Secretary (One each from Boys and Girls school) 


i) He/ She shall be responsible for the social and cultural events undertaken by the Association. 


ii) He/ She shall foster societal interactions amongst the peers.  


iii) He/ She shall keep liaison with Public Relation Secretary and Overseas Secretary. 


4.17 Welfare Secretary 


i) He/ She shall ensure community development and welfare activities undertaken by the Association. 


ii) He/she shall scrutiny the needs assessment in case of medical treatment and propose welfare needed.   


iii) He/ She shall scrutiny the needs assessment in cases of educational support and propose welfare needed.  


iv) He/She shall initiate any proposal of financial welfare in line with the objectives of the Association. 


ARTICLE FIVE: GENERAL MEETING 


5.1 i) There shall be 2 General Meeting of the Association in a year the later shall be treated as the Annual General Meeting with a 30 days notice along with its agenda. 


ii) It shall form committees for any purpose that may be necessary for the functioning or the association. 


iii) General meeting shall enable the forum to amend and generate course of actions for the function of the Association. 


iv) Election Committee shall be elected during the General Meeting. 


v) It shall form committees for any purpose that may be necessary for the functioning or the association. 


5.2 Special General Meeting 


i) Special General Meeting may be called, as and when necessary, by the President. Such meeting shall have at least 7 days notice along with its agenda. 


ii) Special General Meeting empowers the Central Committee the same powers and functions of a General Meeting. 


5.3 Quorum 


i) In case of the Annual General Meeting minimum 35 General members being physically present in the meeting will count as the quorum. 


ii) In case of Special General Meeting minimum 15 General members being physically present in the meeting will count as the quorum. 


5.4-5.9 Reserved 


ARTICLE SIX: FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT 


6.1 The Fund of the Association shall be constituted by Registration Fees, Annual Fees, Voluntary Contributions/ Subscription/ Charity/ Donation/ Grant from 


i) General Members 

ii) Associated Members 

ii) Any Govt Organisation 

iii) Any non-political organization 

iv) Any volunteer  

v) Own initiative/ projects to raise welfare fund 


6.2 The Fund of the Association shall remain deposited in the accounts with scheduled banks. 


6.3 The financial accounts of the Association shall be operated by jointly by the President, Secretary General and the Treasurer of the Association. Any two of them can jointly sign and issue expenditure cheque. 


6.4 The annual financial budget of the Association shall have to be presented and approved in the Annual General Meeting. 


6.5 Any Project to raise own fund, if any shall be operated in accordance with the guidelines and sanctions by the Central committee through a separate bank account maintained in a schedule bank. Project fund refers to such funds and shall be utilized preferably in income generation project for the welfare of the Association. 


6.6 The accounts of the Association shall be audited annually. 


ARTICLES SEVEN: ELECTION COMMISION 

7.1 A five member Election Commission will be formed at least 3 months prior the election. The Central Committee will invite names during the Annual General Meeting.  


7.2 The Election Commission shall not be entitled to contest in the election and after the election the Election Commission will automatically be dissolved. 


7.3 Voting procedure may be determined by the Election Committee, the means may include voting in Ballots/ social network site/ Group Email or through a combination of all means. 


7.4-7.9 Reserved 


ARTICLE EIGHT: AMENDMENT TO THE CONSTITUTION 

8.1 i) Constitutional Amendments can be made in General Meeting or Special General Meeting called duly for the purpose and such amendments must be approved by a 2/3 majority of the Quorum. 

ii) If no such amendments take place, the ongoing Constitution shall undergo a institutional review as decided by the Central Committee. 

8.2 Amendment to the constitution shall 

i) Be signed by the Central Committee 

ii) Any 10 General Member of the quorum. 

8.3-8.9 Reserved 


ARTICLE NINE: SCOPES OF FINANCIAL WELFARE 

9.1 Financial Support for Medical Treatment 

i) In case of a prayer for financial support for medical treatment the Welfare Secretary will make necessary endeavor to scrutiny the gravity of the case vis a vis ability of the applicant. Finally, the needs assessment shall be presented for theperusal of the Central Committee. However, a General Meeting would be required to decide a donation. 

ii) Medical donation is limited to taka 10 lacs. 

iii) In case, the donation requirement exceeds taka 10 lacs, the same might be given on loan or benevolent gratuity basis as decided the Central Committee in a General Meeting. 

iv) This loan is applicable only for the General Members and Associated Members (not being the spouse of the General Member).

9.2 Educational Grant 

i) In case of an application of financial support for education the Welfare Secretary will make necessary endeavor to scrutiny the gravity of the case vis a vis ability of the applicant. Finally, the needs assessment shall be presented for the perusal of the Central Committee. However, a General meeting would be required to decide a grant. 

ii) An educational grant is limited to Taka 5 lacs. 

iii) Financial support on educational ground may exceed but on loan basis. The terms of conditions for the reimbursement will be decided the by Central Committee. 

iv) The educational grant is applicable only for the children of the General Members and Associate Members (not being the spouse of the General member).

9.3 Loan for Financial Insolvency 

i) In case of an application of financial support for meeting financial insolvency, the Welfare Secretary will make necessary endeavor to scrutiny the gravity of the case vis a vis ability of the applicant. Finally, the needs assessment shall be presented for the perusal of the Central Committee.  

ii) A General Meeting would be required to decide a grant. 

iii) Any loan to support an individual is limited to Taka 5 lacs. The terms of conditions for the reimbursement will be decided the by Central Committee. 

iv) This loan is applicable only for the General members and the Associate Members (not  being the spouse of the General member). 

9.4 Any other Prayer 

Any other prayer beyond the prescribed scope would be presented before the Central committee for perusal ad would require a General Meeting. 

9.5-9.9 Reserved 


Chapter TEN: Scope of Connecting People 

10.1 Connecting the friends and keeping them informed of events are of paramount importance. These are critical and challenging too. The committee in general and the concerned Secretaries in particular shall make easy-go approach to keep the connectivity intact. 

i) The prime objective of THIKANA-86 is connecting the peers of 1986 Batch. The ways and means to keep friends connected is of paramount importance. We urge upon the members to keep the data base updated. Hence, the members and the committees are encouraged to avail the blessings of social websites, group mails etc to keep the Association activities progressive. 

ii) An initial data basing and periodical update of records is essential. However, an exhaustive record keeping will best portray an individual and his/her spouse/children with informative details, working domain, blood group, permanent/temporary address etc. 

iii) Nothing in this constitution restricts the concerned Secretary or any Member to generate any ideas for better connectivity and record keeping. 

iv) If any volunteers of other Batches intend to be the part of the Association He/ She may do so through registration of being an Associated Member. 

10.2 -10.5Reserved 

Chapter ELEVEN: Collection of Subscription 

11.1 It may be noted that: 

i) Collecting annual fees/ subscription may best be done through an automated system so that the prescribed annual fee/ subscription of a member is deposited in the central account on a scheduled date of the year. 

ii) Any reimbursement of loan is preferred to be recovered through automated banking system. 

iii) Any other donation/ charity/ funds generated from any project may be deposited in the Central Account as appropriate. 

iv) Financial statement of the account will be presented in the Annual General Meeting will be presented by the Treasurer. 

11.2-11.5 Reserved 

Chapter TWELVE: Review and Amendments 

12.1 The scope of review and amendments are the followings: 

i) This constitution shall undergo an institutional review every 3 years. 

ii) Any amendments as needed for the efficient upkeep of the association shall be concurred by the 2/3 majority in a General Meeting and finally approved by the Central Committee. 

iii) Any incorporation/inclusion which ease outs the administration of the Association may be incorporated in respective reserved articles. 

iv) All members are inspired to put forward suggestions and views so as to enhance the efficiency of the Association, its modality and efficiency. 

12.2-12.5 Reserved
